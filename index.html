STREAMOCRACY

How streaming changed filmaking

In less than twenty years streaming services like Netflix, Prime Video, Hulu and many others have changed the way we watch movies and tv shows, they affected our routines and our culture so thoroughly that they entered in our language!

Binge-watching runner-up for Oxford Dictionary’s “word of the year 2013”

By 2015, "Netflix and chill" has become an Internet meme.

Streaming services challenged the traditional cinemas and television industries by creating new models for the production, distribution, and release of series and movies. The model of Netflix has proven to be the winning formula:

  • a monthly subscription that gives viewers access to the platform's entire content catalogue
  • broadcasting content directly on the web
  • an algorithm that tracks user choices and suggests related content

Their dominant position, places Internet platforms in a strong decisional position in terms of film content. In the future, this kind of authority could be key in determining what constitutes a "well-made film"...

But has streaming really changed the movie industry?

In order to answer our question we start from two existing datasets:

Netflix: one of the most popular media and video streaming platforms. They have over 8000 movies or tv shows available on their platform, as of mid-2021. This tabular dataset consists of listings of all the movies and tv shows available on Netflix.

Amazon prime: one of the most popular media and video streaming platforms. They have close to 10000 movies or tv shows available on their platform, as of mid-2021. This tabular dataset consists of listings of all the movies and tv shows available on Amazon Prime.

We end up with a dataset made by:

8313 titles
5690 directors
1136 distributors
118 countries
163 languages

Starting from our data and our research question: how streaming platforms have changed film production; we decided to divide the analysis into 3 steps:

  1. What kind of content is present on Streaming Platforms?
  2. How do streaming services treat their content?
  3. How do streaming services influence the cinema industry?
Lets start

What kind of content is present on Streaming Platforms?

More movies than you’d expect

Our dataset contains over 8,000 entries from Netflix and Amazon, which covers the movies added since the creation of these platforms to 2021. While they are best known for their TV series, both Amazon and Netflix contain more movies.

Movies from all over the world.

Content comes from all over the world! Despite this, over 40% of the films are from the United States and over 50% of the films are shot in English.

Who records them?

We used the Gender-guesser phyton library to determine the gender of directors based on their names, this library can sometimes fail as it is mainly trained on Anglo-Saxon names, so we have to allow for a margin of error. However, over 80% of all directors turned out to be men. After all, about 4% remain unknown, 12% are women and only 2 authors are registered as transgender females and 2 as non-binary.

Does streaming service have an impact on diversity?

The number of female directors has grown over the years and reached peaks in 2017 and 2020, but probably follows the general trend of film additions that increases over the years and decreases in 2020, most likely due to the impact that the Covid-19 pandemic has had on the entertainment industry world. This means that as women increase, so do men, and the ratio of male to female executives remains essentially unchanged over time.

What about the target?

Movies and TV series follow different classification systems that depend on country, distribution and producers, here we used a grouping system displayed on Amazon Prime Video. We can see that most of the content is aimed at adults in both series and movies, and the amount of content decreases with the age of the target, with the exception of the 13-16 age group, which is very popular targeted by movies and almost completely discarded from TV series.

Now that we understand what these platforms contain, let's dig deeper and ask ourselves: how did these platforms create their movie collections? and how are they managing them?

How do streaming services treat their content?

Netflix over the years

The increase of Netflix's notoriety and success began in 2013, as we can see from the graph, the following years from 2015 to 2020 have a sharp increase in series and especially in movie additions. In 2020 the trend reverses and there is a decline in integrations, this is probably caused by the impact that the covid-19 pandemic has had on film productions, but also by the competition made to Netflix by many other companies that imitate Netflix and reproduce the model. In some cases this meant some major sacrifices for Netflix, for example when Disney + made its debut, it meant the end of Disney blockbusters, like the Star Wars movies, available on Netflix or other platforms.

Amazon over the years

Amazon, on the other hand, has had a more steady trend of movies and series additions since its creation in 2006. Interestingly, in the last year, movie additions have decreased but series additions are on the rise, for likely show a new decision on which content to post.

How does the streaming service affect the entertainment industry?

Who are the distributors?

Our dataset represents data from films hosted on Prime Video and Netflix platforms, the data on distributors refers to the company that initially distributed the title. On streaming platforms, most movies are distributed by the platform itself or by other streaming services such as Hulu. Dominant in the chart is Netflix followed by Amazon. Among the traditional theatrical distributor, only Warner Bros seems to be competitive.

Genres and Distributors

Each distributor has its own personality and favors different genres, Amazon is particularly fond of 'Drama', while the strong point of Netflix are the 'International shows'.

Who wins the prizes?

Netflix and Amazon are not strong distributors of major award-winning films, but both are receiving many lesser-known awards. Until 2020 the rules of the Academy Awards stipulated that a film must have a theatrical release in order to win an Oscar and many films released on streaming platforms were considered "TV Movies". So the question of whether or not streaming services can qualify for certain awards is still being debated by the film community.

... And what prizes do they win?

Due to covid impact on the entertainment business streaming platforms are releasing more and more movies appreciated by the critics and the general public, pushing for the revision of the Academy's rules. Movies distributed by the platforms do not tend to be nominated for awards, with the most acclaimed awards received are coming from TV broadcasters. We will probably have to wait a few years to see any substantial changes.

What does the audience think?

Streaming services connect the way people watch movies. We compare general public opinion (expressed in iMDb reatings) with the critics ones (Rotten Tomatoes). If we look at how these indices evolve over the years, we can see that up until the 1990s both indices followed a similar pattern of peaks and troughs, with critics overall more enthusiastic about films than audiences. Between the 1990s and 2010s, audiences and critics diverged, with audiences more generous with films. Finally, in the last decade, trends have changed again, but both critics and public opinion have increasingly appreciated the films.

Critics over Public

There are several factors that can explain this phenomenon, (for instance both IMDb and Rotten Tomatoes are very recent, so the opinion on old movies is mostly retroactive). Anyway, it is clear that critics’ opinions and public opinions of movies are undergoing a transformation over the last 20 years and it is likely that this also depends on the new ways of movie consumption, introduced by streaming services over the last years.

Conclusion

Has streaming really changed the movie industry?

YES

Streaming services like Netflix and Amazon have created new ways of watching movies and TV series: they have impacted where the contents we watch come from, filling their catalogues with many movies from around the world, and they have had a major impact on the distribution of films becoming a fundamental outlet for cinematic content, they have influenced the awarding of films and are changing the opinion of critics and audiences.

NO

Despite everything, 50% of the films we watch come from the United States and 80% are produced by men. The market, therefore, appears to be Anglo-centric and still not open to diversity.
