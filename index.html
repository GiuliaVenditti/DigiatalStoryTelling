STREAMOCRACY

How streaming changed filmaking

In less than twenty years streaming services like Netflix, Prime Video, Hulu and many others have changed the way we watch movies and tv shows, they affected our routines and our culture so thoroughly that they entered our language.

Binge-watching runner-up for Oxford Dictionary’s “word of the year 2013”

By 2015, Netflix and chill had become an Internet meme.

Streaming services challenged the traditional entertainment industry, by creating new business models for the production, distribution, and release of TV series and movies. The new model includes:

  • monthly subscription plan to access the contents
  • broadcasting content directly on the web
  • algorithm that tracks user choices and suggests related content

Their dominant position, places Internet platforms in a strong decisional position in terms of film content. In the future, this kind of authority could be key in determining what constitutes a "well-made film"...

But has streaming really changed the movie industry?

In oreder to answer our question start from two existing datasets:

Netflix: One of the most popular media and video streaming platforms with over 200M Subscribers. Our Netflix dataset contains data on the over 8000 movies and tv shows available on their platform up to 2021, along with data on: cast, directors, ratings, release year, duration, etc

Amazon prime: popular media and video streaming platforms created by Amazon with over 200M Subscribers globally. Our Prime Video dataset consists of data on all the movies and tv shows available on Amazon Prime Video up to 2021.

We end up with a dataset made by:

8313 titles
6990 directors
6832 distributors
7449 countries
7460 languages

Starting from our data and our research question: how streaming platforms have changed film production; we decided to divide the analysis into 3 steps:

  1. What kind of content is present on Streaming Platforms?
  2. How streaming services treats their content?
  3. How streaming service influence cinema industry?
